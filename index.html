Shahmir Aziz

I am a Rhodes Scholar DPhil student at Oxford's Kavli Institute for Nanoscience Discovery, working with Dr. Dame Carol Robinson on machine learning for nanoscale robotics.

Previously, I graduated from Harvard AB Summa Cum Laude in Mathematics and Biomedical Engineering with a minor in Computer Science. I was awarded the John Harvard Scholar designation and the Detur Prize, the oldest academic prize at Harvard.

I've done quantitative research at Citi's rates trading desk, biological systems optimization at Novo Nordisk, and co-founded SAR Capital Management, an intercollegiate hedge fund focused on biotech equities where I lead quantitative research.

My research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning and nanoscale robotics, particularly in developing ML algorithms for controlling swarm behavior at the molecular level, optimizing targeted drug delivery systems, and creating autonomous nanoscale surgical tools.
